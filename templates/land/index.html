    Most frequently asked questions

    • What is diabetic retinopathy?

      Diabetic retinopathy is a condition related to damage to the retina in the eye as a result of diabetes. High blood sugar levels can cause changes in the blood vessels inside the eye, leading to double vision or even loss of vision in severe cases.

    • What are the common symptoms of diabetic retinopathy?

      Some common symptoms include blurry vision, dark spots in the field of vision (sidesight), changes in night vision, and gradual loss of vision.

    • What factors increase the risk of diabetic retinopathy?

      Factors that increase the risk of developing diabetic retinopathy include having diabetes for a long time, not well-controlled blood sugar levels, high blood pressure, and high levels of cholesterol and fats in the blood.

    • How is diabetic retinopathy diagnosed?

      Diabetic retinopathy is usually diagnosed through eye examinations such as fundus examination (photographic or sonographic) and visual focus examination. You may be asked to visit an ophthalmologist to determine the progress of the disease and determine appropriate treatment.

    • What treatments are available for diabetic retinopathy?

      Treatments available for diabetic retinopathy include effective control of blood sugar levels, healthy lifestyle modifications such as eating healthy food and exercising, and in some severe cases, drug or surgical treatment is used to maintain eye health and preserve vision.
